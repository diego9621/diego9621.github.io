Introduction

Finding the right keywords can be overwhelming at first. However, several effective methods can help you identify potential keywords to drive organic traffic to your website. In this post, I'll share these methods to help you find the best keywords for your content.

Methods for Finding Keywords

Brainstorming

Start by brainstorming words and phrases related to your business or industry. Think about what questions your audience might have.

Competitor Analysis

Analyzing competitors can reveal valuable keywords they’re targeting. Tools like Ahrefs and SEMrush help identify which keywords drive traffic to their sites.

Keyword Research Tools

Using keyword research tools expands your keyword list with data-driven insights.

Google Keyword Planner

A free tool offering keyword ideas and traffic estimates.

Ahrefs

Provides in-depth keyword analysis and competitor insights.

SEMrush

A comprehensive tool for keyword research and SEO analysis.

Ubersuggest

User-friendly with keyword suggestions and competitive data.

Keyword Sheeter

Quickly generates a large list of keywords.

AnswerThePublic

Visualizes search questions and autocomplete searches.

Short-Tail vs. Long-Tail Keywords

Short-Tail Keywords

Broad terms with high search volume and competition (e.g., "shoes").

Long-Tail Keywords

Specific phrases with lower search volume but higher conversion rates (e.g., "best running shoes for flat feet"). Personally, I find long-tail keywords more effective for targeted content.
